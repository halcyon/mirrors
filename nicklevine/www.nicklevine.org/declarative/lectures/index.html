These are the notes I wrote for a lecture course at Anglia Polytechnic University. The official title of the course was "Declarative Languages", but I restricted it to one language - Common Lisp - and subtitled the course

Lisp and elements of style.

You will find notes accompanying all lectures on this site. Solutions for most weeks' work along with some further material have also been added for your edification.. The 1999 and 2000 assignments, along with the January 2000 exam with model answers are also available here.
 

Nick Levine
last modified 2001-09-03

Lecture Notes

  • Week One - To introduce the course, give background, and deal with various administrative issues
  • Week Two - To introduce enough basic concepts that you can start to write simple programs for yourself
  • Week Three - To review carefully old ground, and then pursue a little further some of the concepts which will allow you to write more interesting functions
  • Week Four - Mainly to do with lists...
  • Week Five - Functions, recursive and iterative: the sordid truth and other stuff
  • Week Six - Vectors, strings, characters and keywords
  • Week Seven - Structures, a word about macros, functions of functions, anonymous functions
  • Week Eight - Equality, hash-tables and blocks
  • Week Nine - Catch & throw etc., symbols, and more about functions
  • Week Ten - Variables and flexible argument lists and a number of interesting "that-reminds-me"s
  • Week Eleven - Evaluation rules and macros
  • Week Twelve -  Loose ends / CLOS in 10 minutes / revision quiz / summary
